What is Unseen: Amalgam

The purpose of this page is to show the design of my game and some of the technical design decisions that have been made and implemented, particularly my networking communication design and my adaptive encounter design.

This is a technical showcase of Unseen: Amalgam, a side project I've been building alongside my software engineering studies for the last 1.5 years. It's an online social deduction game inspired by Werewolf, Among Us, and Town of Salem, made in Unity. Currently a fully functional prototype with working multiplayer, a native mobile app for hosting and joining sessions, and a WebGL build for clients.

It's trying to combine what makes games like Werewolf good: you can see each other's faces as you argue and accuse. Digital makes other things possible too, like hidden information, complex role abilities, and atmosphere.

Day Phase

During the day, players debate who to exile and deal with random events: strangers showing up at the door, reports of deaths from the night before, or the group running low on food.

Night Phase

At night, players go out to scavenge for food and supplies. Each journey is private on their own device. They make choices only they see. The Amalgam use this time to kill, sabotage, or keep their cover.

Deception

The core of the game is reading people. When someone comes back from scavenging claiming they found nothing, are they telling the truth? When someone pushes hard to exile a specific player, what's their motive?

Win Conditions

Survivors win by fully repairing a broken radio tower and calling for rescue, or by identifying and exiling every Amalgam. The Amalgam win by killing enough survivors to outnumber them, or by stopping the rescue.

Word of Mouth

Everything players learn at night, they must share out loud. There is no shared log, no kill feed, no notification that a location has already been stripped bare. If you wasted your night scavenging somewhere empty, you have to tell people. If you were out and saw someone from town contort and change into something that is no longer human, you have to say so. The group's survival depends on that information reaching everyone.

But none of it is verified. The Amalgam sit in the same circle, listening and responding. When a player warns the group to stay away from someone they claim to have seen transform, they could be telling the truth — giving the survivors a genuine lead. Or they could be steering attention toward an innocent person, buying themselves another night. There is no way to know except to weigh the player against everything else you've seen from them. That judgment call, made out loud, in front of everyone, is the game.

Technical

Multiplayer Architecture

The game uses a client-host model built on Unity Netcode for GameObjects. One device runs the session as host and holds all the game logic; they're also playing like everyone else. Everyone else connects as clients, either through the native app or a WebGL build in the browser.

Snapshot-Based State Sync

The host keeps the full game state in a single object: resource levels, active Amalgam count, which NPCs in town are secretly Amalgam, noise levels, the current day, who is alive, and what events have already happened.

At the start of each phase, the host packages this into a NightSnapshot and sends a filtered version to each client. A survivor sees different data than the Amalgam. That's how the game keeps information hidden while running everything from one shared state.

Scenario Distribution

During scavenging, the host uses the snapshot to pick scenarios for each player. Scenarios are chosen randomly but weighted by the current state. High noise means more dangerous encounters; low food means more trade opportunities. Every player gets different scenarios, so no two nights play out the same.

Choice Resolution

When a player makes a choice, the client accumulates the results locally throughout the scavenge, then sends a single outcome packet to the host at the end. The host's GameManager processes all incoming changes, resolves role abilities, and updates the master state.

Once all clients have submitted, the host advances the phase and sends out new snapshots. Clients don't know about each other's actions until the host surfaces them, like announcing that someone died during the night.

Walkthrough

Gameplay Flow

One full game loop, from setup to the next morning.

Step 01

Host Creates the Session

The host opens the native app and starts a new game. Everyone else joins from the WebGL build in their browser by entering a room code. No installation needed for clients.

Step 02

Clients Sent to "My Screen"

Players see a personal screen with info about their role and status — HP, items, and any active effects.

Step 03

Roles Are Dealt

Each player checks their phone privately to see their assigned role.

Step 04

Day Phase Begins

Host sees the main day screen with the bunker state: who is alive, what resources are left, and what happened the previous night.

Step 05

Day Encounter

A random encounter tests the group's ability to cooperate. They read it aloud, vote on a response, and the outcome updates the world state — karma, sanity, inventory, or amalgam awareness.

Step 06

Night Falls: Scavenging

Each player is sent to their own private scavenge scene. One draws the supermarket, choosing what to search for food and supplies. Every scavenger sees something different.

Step 07

Role Abilities

The Biologist scans another player — the result shows privately: human or Amalgam. Only they know. They'll decide whether to reveal this during the day discussion.

Step 08

The Amalgam Strikes

The Amalgam opens their private action menu and picks a target. The victim is flagged on the host's GameManager and added to the pending kills list.

Step 09

Morning: Someone Is Dead

Night ends. An alert goes out: one player was found dead. The group has one day of debate before the next night falls. The loop continues until one side wins.

Roles

Player Roles

Each player gets a role at the start. Roles decide which team you're on, Survivor or Amalgam, and give you a unique ability to use during scavenging.

The Survivor

Survivors

No special abilities. Just trying to make it out alive.

The Doctor

Survivors

Can heal injured players during the night phase.

The Soldier

Survivors

Can kill other players during scavenging, but dies too if the target turns out to be a survivor. A powerful ability that comes with real risk.

The Biologist

Survivors

Can scan other scavenging players to determine if they're human or Amalgam.

The Martyr

Survivors

Can choose to pray at night. If attacked while praying, the attacker dies through divine retribution.

The Doomsayer

Amalgam

Believes the Amalgam are the new world order and wants to help them win. Plays on the Amalgam team, but the Biologist's scan reads them as human.

The Amalgam

Amalgam

An alien creature in a human suit. Must kill every survivor without being found out.
